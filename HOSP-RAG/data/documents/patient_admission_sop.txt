STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: PATIENT ADMISSION
Document ID: SOP-ADM-001
Version: 2.1 | Effective Date: January 2024
Department: Admissions & Registration

1. PURPOSE
This SOP outlines the standardized process for admitting patients to the hospital, ensuring accurate registration, proper documentation, and smooth patient flow.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all hospital staff involved in patient admission including front desk, nurses, and administrative staff.

3. PROCEDURE

3.1 EMERGENCY ADMISSIONS
- Triage nurse assesses patient severity using ESI (Emergency Severity Index) scale
- ESI Level 1 (Immediate): Patient taken directly to resuscitation bay
- ESI Level 2 (Emergent): Seen within 15 minutes
- ESI Level 3 (Urgent): Seen within 30 minutes
- ESI Level 4-5 (Less Urgent/Non-urgent): Seen within 60-120 minutes
- Notify attending physician immediately for Level 1 and 2
- Document time of arrival, triage assessment, and initial vitals

3.2 PLANNED/ELECTIVE ADMISSIONS
- Verify pre-admission orders from physician
- Confirm patient insurance eligibility and obtain authorization
- Complete patient registration in HMS (Hospital Management System)
- Collect valid government-issued photo ID and insurance card
- Obtain signed consent forms: General Consent, HIPAA Notice, Financial Agreement
- Assign bed based on clinical need and availability
- Escort patient to assigned unit

3.3 REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION AT ADMISSION
- Patient demographics (full legal name, DOB, address, phone)
- Emergency contact information (name, relationship, phone)
- Insurance information (primary and secondary)
- Referring physician details
- Current medications list
- Known allergies (medication, food, environmental)
- Advance directive status

4. IDENTIFICATION BANDING
- Apply identification wristband immediately upon registration
- Wristband must include: Patient name, DOB, MRN, allergies (in red)
- Verify band accuracy with patient before application
- Replace damaged or illegible bands immediately

5. INSURANCE VERIFICATION
- Run real-time eligibility check through system
- Document copay/deductible amounts
- Collect applicable copay at time of service
- Flag self-pay patients for financial counseling within 24 hours

6. BED ASSIGNMENT
- Contact charge nurse of receiving unit before transfer
- Confirm bed is clean and ready
- Transport patient with appropriate escort (wheelchair/stretcher per condition)
- Complete bed assignment handoff using SBAR format

7. ESCALATION
- Notify supervisor if bed hold exceeds 2 hours
- Contact patient relations for complaints or concerns
- Report any documentation discrepancies to medical records

8. QUALITY INDICATORS
- Target registration time: Under 15 minutes for non-emergency
- Patient identification accuracy: 100%
- Consent completion rate: 100% before treatment
