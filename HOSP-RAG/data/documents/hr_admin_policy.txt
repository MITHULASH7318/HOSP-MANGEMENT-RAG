HOSPITAL POLICY: ADMINISTRATIVE AND HR GUIDELINES
Document ID: POL-HR-001
Version: 3.1 | Effective Date: January 2024
Department: Human Resources & Administration

1. ATTENDANCE AND SCHEDULING

1.1 CALL-OUT PROCEDURE:
- Notify your supervisor at least 2 HOURS before shift start
- Call the unit/department directly (do not text or email only)
- Provide expected return date if known
- Extend call-out if absence continues (each day)

1.2 TARDINESS:
- Arriving more than 7 minutes late is considered tardy
- 3 tardies in 90-day period = written counseling
- Clock in/out using time management system only

1.3 SCHEDULE REQUESTS:
- Submit schedule requests at least 4 weeks in advance
- Holidays requests: 8 weeks in advance
- Use scheduling system (Kronos) for all requests

2. PTO AND LEAVE POLICIES

2.1 PAID TIME OFF (PTO):
- Accrual begins on first day of employment
- Full-time (FT): 18 days/year (Years 1-2), 23 days/year (Years 3-5), 28 days/year (6+ years)
- Part-time (PT): Pro-rated based on hours worked
- Maximum carryover: 5 days into new calendar year

2.2 FMLA (Family Medical Leave Act):
- Eligible after 12 months of employment and 1,250 hours worked
- Up to 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected leave per year
- Medical certification required within 15 days
- Contact HR to initiate FMLA paperwork

2.3 BEREAVEMENT LEAVE:
- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 days paid
- Extended family (grandparent, in-law): 3 days paid
- Submit bereavement documentation to HR

3. MANDATORY TRAINING AND COMPLIANCE

Annual training required by January 31st each year:
□ Fire Safety and Emergency Codes
□ HIPAA Privacy and Security
□ Infection Prevention and Hand Hygiene
□ Workplace Violence Prevention
□ Sexual Harassment Prevention
□ Corporate Compliance
□ Patient Rights and Ethics
□ Safe Patient Handling
□ Information Security Awareness

Consequences for non-completion: Pay hold, suspension, termination

4. DRESS CODE AND APPEARANCE

4.1 CLINICAL STAFF:
- Scrubs in department-assigned color (see color chart)
- Hospital-issued ID badge worn at chest level at all times
- Clean, supportive closed-toe shoes (no sandals)
- Minimal jewelry (no dangling earrings, no rings except plain band)
- Nails: Natural, short, clean. No artificial nails in clinical areas
- Fragrance-free products required in patient care areas

4.2 ADMINISTRATIVE STAFF:
- Business casual minimum
- Hospital ID badge required at all times
- Neat, professional appearance

5. HIPAA COMPLIANCE (Critical for ALL Staff)

5.1 WHAT IS PHI (Protected Health Information)?
Any information that identifies a patient + relates to health condition, care, or payment:
- Name, address, birthdate, Social Security Number
- Medical record number, account number
- Diagnosis, treatment information, test results
- Any photos or information that could identify a patient

5.2 PERMITTED USES OF PHI:
- Treatment (care providers directly involved in patient care)
- Payment (billing, insurance)
- Healthcare Operations (quality improvement, training, auditing)

5.3 PROHIBITED ACTIONS:
- Never access records of patients you are not actively caring for
- Never share patient information with family/friends without authorization
- Never discuss patients in public areas (elevators, cafeteria, hallways)
- Never post patient information on social media
- Never take photos of patients without explicit written consent
- Never leave computer screens with PHI visible/unattended

5.4 HIPAA VIOLATIONS:
- All violations investigated by Compliance Officer
- Violations can result in: Termination, civil penalties up to $50,000/violation, criminal prosecution
- Report suspected violations to Compliance Hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX (anonymous)

6. SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
- Do not post anything about patients, colleagues, or internal hospital matters
- Do not identify your workplace in posts about complaints or incidents
- Assumes ALL posts can become public, even in private groups
- Violations subject to disciplinary action including termination

7. WORKPLACE VIOLENCE PREVENTION
- Report all threats, intimidation, or physical assaults to security (ext. 3333) and supervisor
- Zero tolerance for violence from any source (patients, visitors, staff)
- De-escalation training available quarterly through HR
- Restraint use requires physician order and is subject to strict monitoring

Free, confidential services for all employees:
- Mental health counseling (up to 8 sessions/year)
- Substance abuse support
- Financial counseling
- Legal assistance
- Work-life balance resources
EAP Hotline: 1-800-XXX-0000 (24/7, confidential)
