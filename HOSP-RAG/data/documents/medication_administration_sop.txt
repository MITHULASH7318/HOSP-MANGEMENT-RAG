STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: MEDICATION ADMINISTRATION
Document ID: SOP-MED-003
Version: 3.0 | Effective Date: March 2024
Department: Nursing & Pharmacy

1. PURPOSE
To ensure safe, accurate, and timely medication administration to all patients, preventing medication errors and adverse drug events.

2. THE FIVE RIGHTS OF MEDICATION ADMINISTRATION
Every nurse MUST verify the following before administering any medication:
1. RIGHT PATIENT - Verify using two identifiers (name + DOB or MRN)
2. RIGHT MEDICATION - Check drug name against MAR (Medication Administration Record)
3. RIGHT DOSE - Confirm dose, calculate if needed, double-check high-alert meds
4. RIGHT ROUTE - Oral, IV, IM, SubQ, topical, etc. as ordered
5. RIGHT TIME - Administer within 30-minute window of scheduled time

3. HIGH-ALERT MEDICATIONS (Require Double Verification)
The following medications require TWO nurse verification before administration:
- Insulin (all types)
- Anticoagulants (Heparin, Warfarin, Low Molecular Weight Heparins)
- Opioids (Morphine, Hydromorphone, Fentanyl, Oxycodone)
- Concentrated Electrolytes (KCl, Hypertonic Saline)
- Chemotherapy agents
- Neuromuscular Blocking Agents

4. MEDICATION ADMINISTRATION PROCEDURE

4.1 PREPARATION
- Perform hand hygiene before preparing medications
- Prepare medications in a quiet, well-lit area, free from interruptions
- Never prepare medications for more than one patient at a time
- Check medication expiration date
- Verify allergies before administration

4.2 ADMINISTRATION
- Take medication directly to bedside (do not leave unattended)
- Scan patient wristband barcode (BCMA system)
- Scan medication barcode
- Verify the "5 Rights" at bedside
- Explain medication purpose to patient
- Administer and document immediately in MAR
- Witness patient take oral medications when required

4.3 IV MEDICATION SPECIFIC STEPS
- Verify IV site patency and signs of infiltration/phlebitis
- Check compatibility of IV medications
- Set correct infusion rate on pump
- Label all IV lines with drug name, concentration, rate, date/time

5. MEDICATION ERRORS AND NEAR MISSES
- All errors and near misses must be reported via RL Solutions (incident reporting system) within 1 hour
- Notify charge nurse and attending physician immediately
- Assess and monitor patient for adverse effects
- Document clinical response and interventions

6. CONTROLLED SUBSTANCE MANAGEMENT
- Access only via Pyxis MedStation with valid login
- Waste must be witnessed and co-signed by two nurses
- Count discrepancies must be reported immediately to pharmacy and supervisor
- Never leave controlled substances unattended

7. STORAGE REQUIREMENTS
- Medications stored at room temperature unless refrigeration required
- Refrigerated medications: 2-8°C (36-46°F)
- Controlled substances in double-locked cabinet
- No medications stored in patient's personal belongings without order

8. PATIENT REFUSAL
- Document refusal in MAR
- Educate patient on importance of medication
- Notify prescribing physician
- Never force medication administration
