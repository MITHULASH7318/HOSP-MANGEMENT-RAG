HOSPITAL POLICY: INFECTION PREVENTION AND CONTROL
Document ID: POL-IPC-007
Version: 4.2 | Effective Date: February 2024
Department: All Departments | Owner: Infection Control Committee

1. POLICY STATEMENT
The hospital is committed to preventing healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) through evidence-based practices, staff education, and strict compliance with infection control protocols.

2. HAND HYGIENE (Most Critical Prevention Measure)

2.1 WHEN TO PERFORM HAND HYGIENE (WHO Five Moments):
- BEFORE touching a patient
- BEFORE clean/aseptic procedures
- AFTER body fluid exposure risk
- AFTER touching a patient
- AFTER touching patient surroundings

2.2 HOW TO PERFORM:
ALCOHOL-BASED HAND RUB (preferred when hands not visibly soiled):
- Apply 1-2 pumps to palm
- Rub hands together for 20-30 seconds covering all surfaces
- Allow to dry completely

SOAP AND WATER (required when hands visibly soiled, or after Clostridioides difficile contact):
- Wet hands with water
- Apply soap, lather for at least 20 seconds
- Rinse thoroughly and dry with paper towel
- Use paper towel to turn off faucet

3. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)

3.1 STANDARD PRECAUTIONS (All patients):
- Gloves: When touching blood, body fluids, mucous membranes, non-intact skin
- Gown: When clothing may contact contaminated surfaces/substances
- Mask + Eye Protection: When splashing of blood or body fluids anticipated

3.2 TRANSMISSION-BASED PRECAUTIONS:

CONTACT PRECAUTIONS (MRSA, VRE, C. diff, scabies):
- Dedicated room (or cohort with same pathogen)
- Gloves and gown upon entering room
- Dedicated equipment (stethoscope, BP cuff)
- Green sign on door

DROPLET PRECAUTIONS (Influenza, RSV, pertussis):
- Private room preferred; 3-foot spatial separation minimum
- Surgical mask when within 3 feet of patient
- Patient wears surgical mask during transport
- Yellow sign on door

AIRBORNE PRECAUTIONS (TB, Measles, Varicella, COVID-19 aerosol-generating procedures):
- Negative pressure room (AIIR) required
- N95 respirator (fit-tested) required
- Door must remain closed
- Red sign on door

4. ISOLATION ROOM MANAGEMENT
- Post appropriate precaution sign on room door
- Keep necessary PPE stocked outside room
- Minimize entry to room (bundle care activities)
- Dedicate equipment to isolation rooms
- Perform enhanced terminal cleaning upon patient discharge

5. SHARPS AND BIOHAZARD SAFETY
- Never recap needles (use safety devices)
- Dispose in puncture-resistant sharps containers immediately after use
- Fill containers to maximum ¾ full
- Biohazard waste in red bags
- Report needlestick injuries immediately to occupational health (ext. 4567)

6. SURGICAL SITE INFECTION (SSI) PREVENTION
- Administer prophylactic antibiotics within 60 minutes before incision
- Do not remove hair unless necessary; use clippers, never razors
- Maintain normothermia during surgery
- Maintain blood glucose < 180 mg/dL perioperatively
- Sterile technique throughout procedure

7. CENTRAL LINE ASSOCIATED BLOODSTREAM INFECTION (CLABSI) PREVENTION
- Maximal barrier precautions during insertion (cap, mask, sterile gown/gloves, full body drape)
- Chlorhexidine skin antisepsis
- Subclavian or internal jugular preferred over femoral
- Daily review of line necessity; remove promptly when no longer needed
- Change dressing every 7 days or when soiled/loose

8. CATHETER ASSOCIATED URINARY TRACT INFECTION (CAUTI) PREVENTION
- Insert only when medically necessary
- Maintain closed drainage system
- Secure catheter to prevent movement
- Keep drainage bag below bladder level
- Empty bag regularly using aseptic technique
- Assess need daily; remove as soon as possible

9. OUTBREAK MANAGEMENT
- Report clusters of infections to Infection Control immediately
- Implement enhanced surveillance
- Restrict affected staff from patient care
- Notify Public Health Department for notifiable diseases
