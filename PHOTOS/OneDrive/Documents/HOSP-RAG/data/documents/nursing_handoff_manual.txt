CLINICAL MANUAL: NURSING HANDOFF AND COMMUNICATION STANDARDS
Document ID: MAN-NUR-012
Version: 1.8 | Effective Date: April 2024
Department: Nursing | Owner: Chief Nursing Officer

1. PURPOSE
To standardize nursing handoff communication to ensure patient safety, continuity of care, and accurate information transfer between shifts and care providers.

2. SBAR COMMUNICATION FRAMEWORK

SBAR is the standardized communication tool used for ALL clinical handoffs and urgent communications.

S - SITUATION (What is happening?)
- Identify yourself (name, unit, role)
- Patient name, age, MRN
- Current concern or reason for communication
Example: "I'm Nurse Sarah from 4 North. I'm calling about Mr. Johnson, 67-year-old in room 412, MRN 789654. He's developed sudden onset shortness of breath."

I - BACKGROUND (What is the clinical background?)
- Admission diagnosis and date
- Relevant medical history
- Current medications and allergies
- Recent procedures or lab results
Example: "He was admitted 2 days ago with community-acquired pneumonia. History of COPD and hypertension. Currently on IV Piperacillin-Tazobactam and Prednisone."

A - ASSESSMENT (What do I think the problem is?)
- Vital signs (current)
- Relevant assessment findings
- Your clinical impression
Example: "Vitals: SpO2 dropped to 88% on 2L NC, RR 24, HR 108, BP 142/88, Temp 38.1°C. Decreased breath sounds on right. I'm concerned about worsening pneumonia or possible pleural effusion."

R - RECOMMENDATION (What do I want?)
- Specific request or action needed
- Urgency level
- What you've already done
Example: "I've increased O2 to 4L NC with improvement to 93%. I'd like you to come evaluate the patient. Do you want a STAT chest X-ray and ABG?"

3. SHIFT HANDOFF (BEDSIDE REPORT)

3.1 WHEN:
- End of every shift (30 minutes before shift end)
- Patient transfers between units
- Handoff to procedure/OR/radiology teams

3.2 BEDSIDE REPORT PROCESS:
- Both nurses go to patient's room together
- Introduce oncoming nurse to patient
- Conduct handoff at bedside using electronic MAR and care plan
- Involve patient in report (ask about concerns, pain level, questions)
- Oncoming nurse performs visual assessment during report
- Review: Active orders, upcoming procedures, pending labs/results
- Critical values or safety concerns communicated FIRST

3.3 REQUIRED ELEMENTS IN HANDOFF:
□ Patient name, room, MRN, attending physician
□ Admission diagnosis and date
□ Current vital signs and trend
□ Pain level (current and goal)
□ Diet and activity orders
□ IV access (location, gauge, date inserted)
□ Current IV fluids and medications due
□ Scheduled and PRN medications given last shift
□ Pending labs, imaging, consults
□ Outstanding tasks or follow-up needed
□ Code status and advance directives
□ Fall risk level and safety measures
□ Skin integrity / wound status
□ Isolation precautions
□ Anticipated discharge plan

4. CRITICAL VALUE REPORTING
When laboratory or radiology reports a critical value:
- Write down the value and time received
- Notify physician/provider within 30 MINUTES
- Use SBAR format
- Document: Time value received, time physician notified, physician name, orders received
Critical value examples: Na <120 or >160, K <2.5 or >6.5, Glucose <40 or >500, Troponin positive, PT/INR >5, Platelet <20,000

5. ESCALATION - RAPID RESPONSE TEAM (RRT)
Call RRT (ext. 5555) for ANY of the following:
- Acute change in mental status
- Respiratory distress (RR >28 or <8)
- SpO2 <90% unresponsive to intervention
- Systolic BP <90 or >200 mmHg
- HR <40 or >130 bpm
- Nurse is concerned about patient condition
IMPORTANT: Any nurse can activate RRT without physician order. Never delay for fear of "bothering" anyone.

6. MEDICATION RECONCILIATION
- Complete full medication reconciliation within 24 hours of admission
- Verify with patient, family, and previous pharmacy/provider records
- Compare home medications to current orders; document discrepancies
- Repeat reconciliation at every transition of care and at discharge

7. DISCHARGE PLANNING
- Begin discharge planning at admission
- Assess needs: Transportation, home support, equipment, follow-up appointments
- Patient education: Diagnosis, medications, warning signs, follow-up
- Provide written discharge instructions in appropriate language
- Teach-back method to confirm understanding
- Discharge summary to PCP within 24 hours

8. DO NOT USE ABBREVIATIONS
The following abbreviations are PROHIBITED in clinical documentation:
- U (unit) → Write "unit"
- IU (international unit) → Write "international unit"
- QD, QOD → Write "daily", "every other day"
- Trailing zero (1.0 mg) → Write "1 mg"
- Naked decimal (.5 mg) → Write "0.5 mg"
- MS, MSO4, MgSO4 → Write "morphine sulfate" or "magnesium sulfate"
